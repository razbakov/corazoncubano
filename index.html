the other Munich week

Europe's refuge for Cuban culture — in Munich.

The world thinks Munich means Oktoberfest. We're building the counter-week. For seven days in July, Munich beats with a Cuban heart — music, dance, food, language, art. A home for a culture that travels light but needs a place to land.

Refuge is not asylum. Refuge is a place where Cuban music plays year-round, where Cuban food sits on a Bavarian table, where Spanish flows in a Munich bar at 1am. We are eleven friends building that place — one festival, one bar, one community at a time.

now picture it

The Wiese, reimagined.

The Theresienwiese — Munich's 420,000 m² festival ground where Oktoberfest stands eleven months a year. For one week each July, we picture it like this.

El Malecón
golden hour · Munich gets a sea
A turquoise channel cuts across the Wiese. People sit on the seawall, music spills from coral bars, vintage Chevrolets at the edges. The Bavaria statue is the sunset horizon.
Tropicana
blue hour · cabaret under Bavaria
Open-air stage with the legendary neon sign. Amber lamps on every dinner table, palms framing the orchestra. The Bavaria statue lit and watching the dancers.
Plaza Vieja
afternoon · a corner of Habana, transplanted
Pastel colonial facades — coral, turquoise, ochre, mint. Cobblestones, salsa dancers in the square, a fruit cart with mangoes. The Bavaria statue rises behind the rooftops as cultural contrast.
Habana on the Wiese
twilight aerial · the master plan view
A pop-up Cuban quarter from above. The Capitolio dome at the center, neon Floridita and Bodeguita signs, palms and string lights. Bavaria marks the boundary.
